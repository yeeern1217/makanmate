## Deck content, speaker script, and image-generation brief

**Total pitch time:** ~6 minutes (14 slides, ~25-30s each)
**Slide format:** 16:9 widescreen
**Visual style anchor (use on EVERY slide):** Malaysian-heritage retro illustration — warm terracotta red (#8B1E1E), deep leaf green (#2F5D3A), cream/parchment background (#F4ECD8), with hand-drawn batik-inspired ornaments, vintage hawker-stall iconography (woks, charcoal, kopitiam cups, banana-leaf plates). ## NARRATIVE ARC (for the image model's context)

A tourist lands in Penang. They cannot read the handwritten Malay menu at a roadside stall. They have a shellfish allergy. They walk away. The hawker loses a customer; the tourist eats at Starbucks.

**MakanMate is the layer between the phone and the handwritten board.** A 4-layer agentic pipeline — Vision → Personalization → Heritage RAG → Critic Audit — turns any unmarked stall into a safe, narrated, discoverable experience. No new hardware. No reprinted menus.

The punchline for judges: **the intelligence about which stalls matter doesn't live in any foundation model — it lives in our data.** We deliberately invert recommendation logic: fewer online reviews = higher rank. The invisible uncle beats the viral cafe.

**Why this is dangerous:** it's a "you didn't think this through" question. The answer has to show the flow serves multiple distinct purposes, not just anti-fraud.
